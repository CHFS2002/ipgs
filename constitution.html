Constitution of the IPGS

Adopted at the Founding Meeting, Wuhan, 8 May 2026.

Preamble

Plant grafting is one of humanity's oldest agricultural technologies and one of its most scientifically underexplored. As climate change, soilborne disease pressure, and the need for resource-efficient food systems intensify, the global research community requires a dedicated, open, and collaborative institutional home. The International Plant Grafting Society (IPGS) is established to provide that home — uniting researchers, institutions, and disciplines across all continents in the shared pursuit of grafting science for a resilient global food system.

Article I — Name, Seat, and Language

1.1 The Society shall be known as the International Plant Grafting Society, abbreviated IPGS.

1.2 The registered Secretariat of the Society shall be located at the College of Horticulture & Forestry Sciences (CHFS), Huazhong Agricultural University (HZAU), Wuhan, Hubei, China.

1.3 The Society is a purely academic, non-commercial organisation. It shall not engage in commercial activity or distribute financial surplus to its members.

1.4 The official language of the Society shall be English. Documents, communications, and proceedings shall be conducted in English unless otherwise decided by the Board for specific regional purposes.

Article II — Objectives

The Society shall pursue the following objectives:

  1. Advance the scientific understanding of plant grafting — its molecular, physiological, and agronomic dimensions.
  2. Coordinate international collaborative research through joint programs, Working Groups, and shared infrastructure.
  3. Establish and maintain open-access knowledge resources, protocols, and datasets for the global research community.
  4. Train the next generation of grafting scientists through fellowships, summer institutes, and mobility programs.
  5. Promote evidence-based standards and regulatory frameworks for grafted-plant production worldwide.
  6. Strengthen linkages between academic research and the grafting industry.
  7. Engage with FAO, CGIAR, and national bodies to ensure grafting science informs global food and agriculture policy.

Article III — Membership

3.2 Founding Member status is permanent and irrevocable. For the inaugural Board (2026–2030), Co-Chair and Regional Director positions shall be filled by Founding Members or their nominated institutional representatives. Subsequent terms are subject to election by the General Assembly under Article V.

3.3 Full and Associate membership applications are submitted to the Secretariat and approved by a simple majority of the Board. The Board shall publish and maintain membership criteria consistent with the Society's objectives.

3.4 Observer status is granted by Board resolution to intergovernmental and national bodies upon written request.

3.5 All members are expected to uphold the Society's Code of Conduct (Article XIV), participate in Working Group activities, and contribute to the Knowledge Commons to the extent their institutional policies allow.

3.6 Membership dues, if any, shall be set by the Board and ratified by the General Assembly. For the founding period (2026–2028), no institutional dues shall be levied.

3.7 Membership may be suspended or terminated by a two-thirds majority of the Board for serious or persistent breach of the Code of Conduct. The affected member shall be given 30 days to respond before a final decision is taken.

Article IV — Governance

4.1 The Society shall be governed by three bodies:

  • The General Assembly
  • The Board of Directors
  • The Secretariat

4.2 General Assembly

4.2.1 The General Assembly consists of all Founding and Full Members. Each holds one vote.

4.2.2 The General Assembly shall meet at least once every two years, ordinarily at the International Plant Grafting Symposium. Extraordinary sessions may be convened by the Board or upon written request of one-third of voting members. Meetings may be held in person, by video conference, or in hybrid format.

4.2.3 The General Assembly shall: elect the Chair and Co-Chairs; approve the biennial work plan and budget; ratify amendments to this Constitution; and dissolve the Society if required.

4.2.4 Decisions require a simple majority of voting members present, represented by proxy, or participating remotely, except for Constitutional amendments (Article XII) and dissolution (Article XIII).

5.2 The Chair is elected by the General Assembly from among Founding or Full Members. Co-Chairs are elected by the General Assembly on nomination by their respective regional member institutions. Where no member institution exists in a region, the Board may appoint an interim Co-Chair until a regional election is possible.

5.3 Secretaries shall comprise four administrative staff based at the Secretariat and one Regional Secretary per Co-Chair region. Regional Secretaries support their respective Co-Chair in regional coordination, membership outreach, and event organisation. All Secretary responsibilities shall be further defined in the Board's Rules of Procedure.

5.4 The General Secretary is based at the College of Horticulture & Forestry Sciences, HZAU, serving ex officio for the duration of the Secretariat hosting agreement.

5.5 Board decisions are taken by simple majority vote of voting Directors present or participating remotely. The Chair holds a casting vote in the event of a tie. Quorum requires five voting Directors.

5.6 The Board shall meet at least twice per year. Meetings may be held in person, by video conference, or in hybrid format; remote participation counts toward quorum.

5.7 Directors may be removed by a two-thirds vote of the General Assembly for conduct inconsistent with the Society's Code of Conduct or persistent failure to fulfil their duties.

5.8 Elections Procedure. The Board shall establish and publish a written Elections Procedure within six months of founding, covering nomination timelines, eligibility, voting method, and proxy arrangements. The procedure shall be reviewed at each General Assembly.

5.9 Conflict of Interest. Any Director who has a direct financial, professional, or personal interest in a matter before the Board shall declare that interest at the earliest opportunity and shall withdraw from discussion and voting on that matter. Declarations shall be recorded in the minutes.

Article VI — Secretariat

6.1 The Secretariat is hosted at HZAU-CHFS, Wuhan, and is responsible for: day-to-day administration; maintenance of the membership register; organisation of events; management of Society communications and the website; and custody of official records.

6.2 The hosting arrangement between IPGS and HZAU shall be formalised in a Memorandum of Understanding, renewable every five years. Relocation of the Secretariat requires a two-thirds vote of the General Assembly.

Article VII — Working Groups

7.1 The Society shall maintain seven thematic Working Groups:

  1. Grafting Biology
  2. Rootstock Improvement
  3. Scion–Rootstock Interactions
  4. Horticultural Systems & Crop Performance
  5. Phytosanitary Standards & Regulatory Affairs
  6. Knowledge Commons & Data Standards
  7. Capacity Building & Technology Transfer

7.2 Each Working Group is led by a Head appointed by the Board for a two-year term, renewable twice. The Head must be a Founding, Full, or Associate Member.

7.3 Working Groups may establish sub-groups, develop their own work plans, and invite external experts, subject to Board approval.

7.4 The Board may establish, merge, or dissolve Working Groups by a two-thirds majority, subject to ratification by the General Assembly.

Article VIII — Research Framework

8.1 The Society operates a three-tier collaborative research framework:

3Flagship Programs4–5 yearsMulti-WG · multi-country

8.2 The Society shall maintain an IPGS Fellowship & Mobility Fund to support Tier 1 seed grants and researcher mobility (doctoral exchanges and postdoctoral placements). The Fund shall be capitalised from institutional contributions, competitive grants, and donations. Its use shall be governed by Terms of Reference approved by the Board and audited annually.

8.3 All IPGS-supported research outputs shall be deposited in the Knowledge Commons under an open-access licence wherever practicable.

Article IX — Knowledge Commons

9.1 The Society shall maintain a Knowledge Commons — an open-access repository of datasets, protocols, preprints, and educational resources arising from IPGS-supported activities.

9.2 Data and protocols shall be assigned persistent identifiers (DOIs) and MIGE-compliant metadata wherever practicable.

9.3 Joint publications shall aim for Gold or Diamond open access where funding and journal options allow.

9.4 Personal data and commercially sensitive information are excluded from open release and handled under a separate data-protection policy approved by the Board.

Article X — Events and Programs

10.1 The Society shall organise or co-organise the following recurring programs:

  • International Plant Grafting Symposium — biennial; flagship scientific and governance event, including the General Assembly and the IPGS Awards ceremony.
  • Annual Grafting Science Workshop — intensive single-theme workshop co-organised by at least two member institutions.
  • HZAU Grafting Summer Institute — annual two-week residential training program for MSc students and early-career researchers nominated by member institutions.
  • Doctoral Fellowship Program — funded 3–6 month laboratory exchanges for PhD students enrolled at member institutions.
  • Postdoctoral Mobility Program — funded placements at partner member institutions for postdoctoral researchers.

10.2 IPGS Awards. The Society shall confer awards at each International Plant Grafting Symposium to recognise outstanding contributions to grafting science, early-career achievement, and international collaboration. Award categories and criteria shall be defined by the Board.

Article XI — Finances

11.1 The Society's funds shall derive from: membership contributions (if levied); competitive grants; donations and endowments from non-commercial sources; event surpluses; and institutional support agreements.

11.2 The Board shall approve an annual budget and present audited accounts to the General Assembly. Accounts shall be audited annually by an independent auditor appointed by the Board.

11.3 Audited financial statements shall be made available to all voting members within three months of the close of each financial year.

11.4 No part of the Society's income or assets shall be distributed to members except as reimbursement of legitimate and properly documented expenses.

Article XII — Amendments

12.1 Amendments to this Constitution may be proposed by the Board or by written petition of one-third of voting members.

12.2 Proposed amendments shall be circulated to all voting members at least 60 days before the General Assembly at which they are to be considered. In exceptional circumstances, the Board may reduce this notice to 30 days, in which case a three-quarters majority shall be required for adoption.

12.3 Adoption under standard procedure requires a two-thirds majority of voting members present, represented by proxy, or participating remotely.

Article XIII — Dissolution

13.1 The Society may be dissolved by a resolution of the General Assembly passed by a three-quarters majority of all voting members.

13.2 Upon dissolution, any remaining assets, after settlement of liabilities, shall be transferred to one or more organisations with compatible scientific objectives, as determined by the final General Assembly.

Article XIV — Code of Conduct

14.1 The Board shall adopt a Code of Conduct within six months of founding, setting standards for professional behaviour, scientific integrity, and respectful engagement among all members and at all Society events.

14.2 The Code of Conduct may be amended by Board resolution and shall be published on the Society's website. All members are bound by it as a condition of membership.

14.3 Complaints regarding breaches of the Code of Conduct shall be submitted to the Secretariat and reviewed by a panel appointed by the Board, which shall be independent of any party to the complaint.

Article XV — Entry into Force

15.1 This Constitution enters into force upon signature by the Founding Members at the IPGS Founding Meeting, Wuhan, 8 May 2026.

15.2 The Board shall adopt Rules of Procedure governing the detailed conduct of meetings, elections, and Working Group operations within six months of founding.
